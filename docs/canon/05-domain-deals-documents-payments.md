# 05 - Domain: Deals, Documents, and Payments

## 1. Domain Overview
The **Deals, Documents, and Payments** domain governs the transactional lifecycle of a vehicle purchase. This domain connects customers to specific vehicles and manages the multi-step progression from initial interest to final delivery.

Key entities include:
*   **Deal:** The central transaction record.
*   **DealDocument:** Individual files or signatures required for the sale.
*   **DealDeposit:** Reservation payments processed via Stripe.
*   **DocuSignEnvelope:** Tracking for contract packages sent for digital signature.

## 2. Deal Entity
The **Deal** is the primary orchestrator for the purchasing flow, linking a customer and a vehicle into a unique transaction.

### Key Fields
*   `id`: Unique identifier (UUID).
*   `vehicle_id`: Foreign key to the `Vehicle` record.
*   `customer_id`: Foreign key to the `Customer` record.
*   `deal_status`: Enum representing the current stage (see Section 3).
*   `purchase_price`: Total agreed-upon sales price (USD).
*   `deposit_amount`: The specific amount paid as a reservation (USD).
*   `created_at`: Timestamp of deal initiation.

### Ownership
*   Deals are created and managed by the **Owner**.
*   Customers have read-only access to their own deals.

## 3. Deal Lifecycle
The `deal_status` governs the progression of the transaction.

### States & Transitions
*   **Lead:** Interest recorded (e.g., from an inquiry or abandoned deposit).
*   **Deposit Pending:** Customer has initiated the reservation payment but not yet confirmed.
*   **Deposit Received:** Stripe confirmation received. Vehicle is moved to `Reserved`.
*   **Documents Pending:** Waiting for customer to upload ID and insurance documents.
*   **Contracts Sent:** Owner has generated and sent DocuSign contracts.
*   **Contracts Signed:** Customer has completed all digital signatures.
*   **Financing Pending:** Manual stage for finalizing funding/liens (outside system).
*   **Ready for Delivery:** All requirements met; pickup/delivery is scheduled.
*   **Completed:** Vehicle delivered and transaction finalized. Vehicle moved to `Sold`.
*   **Cancelled:** Deal terminated (see Section 9).

### Customer Visibility
*   Customers see their deal's status in the portal with simplified, trust-building labels (e.g., "Vehicle Reserved" for `Deposit Received`).

## 4. Deal Deposit Entity
Represents the $500–$1000 reservation deposit processed via Stripe.

### Key Fields
*   `id`: Unique identifier.
*   `deal_id`: Foreign key to the associated `Deal`.
*   `stripe_payment_id`: Reference to the Stripe transaction.
*   `deposit_amount`: Amount in USD.
*   `payment_status`: Enum (`PENDING`, `SUCCEEDED`, `FAILED`, `REFUNDED`).
*   `payment_timestamp`: When the payment was confirmed.

## 5. Stripe Deposit Rules
*   **Amount:** Typically $500–$1000.
*   **Vehicle Reservation:** Successful payment immediately marks the vehicle as `Reserved` and the deal as `Deposit Received`.
*   **Purchase Credit:** The deposit amount is explicitly credited toward the final purchase price in the settlement statement.
*   **Manual Refunds:** All refunds are initiated manually by the Owner via the Stripe dashboard/API to ensure compliance with dealership policy.

## 6. Deal Documents
Documents required for the sale can be manually uploaded by the customer or generated by the owner.

### Entity: DealDocument
*   `id`: Unique identifier.
*   `deal_id`: Foreign key to the `Deal`.
*   `document_type`: e.g., `Purchase Agreement`, `Drivers License`, `Proof of Insurance`.
*   `document_status`: Enum (`PENDING`, `UPLOADED`, `VERIFIED`, `REJECTED`).
*   `file_url`: Private link to the document storage (S3/Signed URL).
*   `created_at`: Timestamp.

## 7. DocuSign Envelope Model
Represents a package of documents sent for digital signature.

### Entity: DocuSignEnvelope
*   `id`: Unique identifier.
*   `deal_id`: Foreign key to the `Deal`.
*   `envelope_id`: The external ID provided by DocuSign.
*   `envelope_status`: Enum (`SENT`, `DELIVERED`, `COMPLETED`, `VOIDED`).
*   `sent_at`: Timestamp of initiation.
*   `completed_at`: Timestamp when all parties have signed.

### Logic
*   When the envelope status becomes `COMPLETED`, the deal state automatically transitions to `Contracts Signed`.

## 8. Document Access Control
*   **No Public Access:** No documents are served via public URLs.
*   **Restricted Retrieval:** File access is granted only via:
    *   The **Owner** account.
    *   The **Customer** associated with the `deal_id`.
*   **Cross-Deal Isolation:** Customers must not be able to guess or access document IDs for other deals.

## 9. Cancellation Rules
Deals may be moved to the `Cancelled` state for several reasons:
*   **Customer Decline:** Customer decides not to proceed after deposit (Owner manually handles refund).
*   **Vehicle Unavailable:** Sourced vehicle becomes unavailable before purchase is finalized.
*   **Failure to Complete:** Customer fails to provide documents or sign contracts within the specified timeframe.
*   **Owner Discretion:** Owner cancels the deal for operational reasons.

### Vehicle State on Cancellation
*   When a deal is `Cancelled`, the associated vehicle status must be reverted to `Listed` or `Archived` as appropriate to prevent "locking" inventory.

## 10. Customer Visibility Rules
**Customers can see:**
*   Current deal status and milestone progress.
*   The amount of deposit paid and credited.
*   Their uploaded identification and insurance documents.
*   Signed PDF copies of finalized contracts.
*   The total purchase price and remaining balance.

**Customers cannot see:**
*   Internal dealer notes on the deal.
*   Internal document verification logs.
*   Stripe metadata or internal DocuSign logs.
